Proof boundary

What is proven here

The core claim is that workflows which externalize authority into explicit, versioned contracts exhibit lower iterative drift than workflows that modify code directly through conversational prompts.

This site argues for a repeatable mechanism and a bounded research claim, not a universal proof about every model, team, or software domain.

What remains bounded

  • This is a bounded engineering research corpus, not a universal benchmark claim.
  • The current evidence is strongest on invariant-scope drift under iterative change.
  • The research argues for authority structure as the differentiator, not raw model capability.

What this work does not claim

  • It does not claim every AI-assisted workflow fails.
  • It does not claim all prompts are useless.
  • It does not claim statistical closure across every model, organization, or domain.
  • It does not replace the broader conceptual framing in {{CCE_TITLE}}.

The closure move here is explicit boundedness: a clear claim, a clear evidence type, and a clear limit surface.
