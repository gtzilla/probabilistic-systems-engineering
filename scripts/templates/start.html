What this site is

{{SITE_NAME}}

Probabilistic Systems Engineering is a bounded body of work about preserving decision authority when AI makes implementation cheap, fast, and difficult to safely review.

The site has four layers: authority essays that explain where control lives, papers that make the engineering claims legible, contracts that expose the source of truth, and replication materials kept for portability, rerun support, and verification.

Do not start with replication. It exists so the work can be rerun, checked, and transported. It is not the primary cold-reading path.

Terms for humans

Authority
The place where a system can actually permit, refuse, or constrain action.
Contract
An explicit source of truth for what must stay true across change.
Invariant
A rule that must continue to hold after a change.
Decision surface
The place where acceptance, persistence, execution, or refusal actually occurs.
Same-surface change
A change that touches only the place named in the request or prompt.
Cross-surface invariant
A rule that must hold across multiple mutation paths, not just one named location.
Replication
Portable rerun material for checking or transporting the work.
Proof-of-mechanism
Evidence that a repeatable failure mechanism exists, not a claim of universal statistical proof.
